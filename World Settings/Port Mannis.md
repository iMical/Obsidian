	 
Main Plot Setting for the story
Hometown of the Hills family

Port Mannis is a town bordering the eastern coast of what is now known as North America. The town specializes in Whaling, the act of hunting, cleansing, and harvesting whales for blubber. As a result of its great returns in blubber, many countries invest into Port Mannis as a means to create an investment in candles and ointments that aid against recent plague resurgences.

Port Mannis has a small population of 2,257 civilians, working together to ensure prolific harvests. Due to it's small population, Port Mannis is tightly bound, acting as one giant family. As such, the town is described almost as a miniature city, because of its high efficiency work ethic and recent urbanization achievements.

Port Mannis has recently been the face of many newspapers, spouting about the power of witchcraft, that of which challenge the powers of the Gods that watch over the world. Sarah Hills, the only daughter of the Hills family and an amateur reporter follows the story of the Port Mannis witch, discovering that there is much more within Port Mannis than she originally believed.
