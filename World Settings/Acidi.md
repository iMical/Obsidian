
Secondary plot setting for the story
The hometown of Maria Hizuru

Acidi is a town bordering the western coast of what is now known as Asia.  Acidi's market specializes in minerals, it's land rich in silvers, coppers, diamonds, and golds. It is a place of interest for many towns because of this.

Despite the distance between itself and Port Mannis, the two towns are closely related to one another, often engaging in trade. This is, Acidi's interest in whale blubber for candles to use in mining expeditions, and Port Mannis' interest in minerals to forge weaponry used for whaling.

Acidi has a small population, at 1,482 civilians, working together as miners to ensure enough material for international trade. This tight bound calls for Acidi's family-like bond, resulting in high work efficiency and morale. 

Behind Acidi's seemingly neverending conquest in the mines, there is a small lineage of witch hunters living within Acidi, that of which has not been active since the disappearance of The Gods.
